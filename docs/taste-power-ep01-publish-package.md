## YouTube Description (120–180 words)
Black pepper sits quietly on your table today—but for centuries, it powered empires, bankrolled merchants, and reshaped global trade. In this episode of *Taste & Power*, we trace pepper’s journey from India’s Malabar Coast to Rome and Venice, where peppercorns were treated like currency and fortunes were built on spice routes.

Then everything changed: the fall of Constantinople, the scramble for new sea routes, and the rise of Portugal and the Dutch East India Company turned pepper into the center of a global economic war. What started as a kitchen ingredient helped trigger exploration, monopoly battles, and the foundations of modern capitalism.

If you enjoy history, trade, and the hidden stories behind everyday life, subscribe for more episodes on the ingredients that shaped civilization.
