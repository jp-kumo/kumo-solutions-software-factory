# Knowledge Capture — “7 Boring Micro-SaaS Ideas” (Blue-Collar Ops)

Captured: 2026-03-03

## Core thesis
Build “painkiller” SaaS for operationally stressed, cash-generating local businesses (inspectors, HVAC, staffing, legal intake, real estate media, contractors, consignment shops), not hype products for developers.

## Relevance to Jacques / Kumo
Strong fit with Kumo’s current direction: practical automation with measurable ROI and recurring retainers.

## High-signal ideas from article
1. Home inspector tap-to-report (offline mobile + snippets + photo markup)
2. HVAC compliance automation (hard-stop workflow + audit export)
3. Staffing shift-bidding via SMS (Twilio + fairness logic)
4. Estate-law intake portal (validation + secure docs + CRM integration)
5. Real-estate photographer delivery hub (branded/unbranded + lead form)
6. Contractor order app (one-click favorites + local vendor SKUs)
7. Consignment payout tracker (inventory aging + payout math)

## Pattern that matters
- Sell risk/time reduction, not software features.
- Add domain-specific “moat” data/rules (templates, banned lists, compliance fields, SKU catalogs).
- Distribution through native channels (brokers, paralegals, footer virality, vendor floor outreach).

## Best fit for immediate Kumo productization
### Option A — HVAC Compliance Sidecar
- Why: high financial/regulatory pain, easy ROI narrative.
- MVP: required-field hard-stop + OCR model label scan + one-click audit export.

### Option B — Meeting/Notes/Task Ops for service businesses
- Why: aligns with existing Kumo automations and fastest build path.
- MVP: transcript -> tasks -> reminders -> weekly report.

### Option C — Lead intent + response workflow for small contractors
- Why: direct revenue impact, low-friction implementation.
- MVP: classify inbound messages, prioritize intent, SLA reminders.

## 2-week validation play
1. Call 5 local businesses in one vertical using pain-validation script.
2. Confirm time/cost burden in minutes/day and missed revenue impact.
3. Offer a paid 14-day pilot with one measurable KPI.
4. Convert pilot to monthly retainer with tiered scope.

## Anti-hype guardrails
- No “AI magic” claims.
- Promise operational outcome and prove with before/after metrics.
- Keep compliance language clear and conservative.
