# AI Risk Management: Thinking Beyond Regulatory Boundaries

**Source file:** AI_Risk_Management_Research_Release_Presentation.pptx---45cabdc3-330a-4e63-9d05-cd82815451fb.pdf  
**Release date (from deck):** November 14, 2024  
**Group:** AI Governance and Compliance Working Group

## Key points captured
- AI integration creates upside (efficiency, innovation) and material risk exposure.
- Auditing should be lifecycle-based and go beyond minimum regulatory compliance.
- Risk assessment should be impartial, dynamic, and tailored to business context.
- Focus areas: privacy, security, trustworthiness, and investigative/critical thinking.
- AI resilience is framed as:
  - robustness (withstand threats)
  - resilience (recover)
  - plasticity (adapt and improve)
- Governance expectations include traceability of changes and a kill-switch style control for dysfunction.

## Portfolio relevance
This aligns directly with production AI governance narratives:
- beyond-compliance risk design
- lifecycle controls across development/deployment/operations
- resilience engineering mindset for AI systems
- auditability and operational fail-safe mechanisms

## Applied implementation checklist
1. Define lifecycle audit checkpoints for every AI use case.
2. Add trustworthiness criteria (privacy, security, explainability, performance) to release gates.
3. Build traceability for model/prompt/data/config changes.
4. Add incident response playbooks and controlled rollback/kill-switch path.
5. Evaluate resilience posture across robustness, recovery, and adaptation.
6. Require business-context risk tailoring (no one-size-fits-all audit template).
