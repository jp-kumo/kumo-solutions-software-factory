# Kumo Solutions — 2-Minute Sales Call Cheat Sheet

Use this in discovery calls and DMs. Keep it practical and direct.

---

## 30-Second Opener

“Quick context: Kumo automates repetitive ops and content work for small teams. We usually start with a 14-day pilot so you can see ROI before committing to a retainer.”

---

## 5 Discovery Questions

1. What repetitive task is currently draining your team every week?
2. How many hours/week are spent on that today?
3. What happens when this work is delayed or missed?
4. What tools do you already use (email, Slack, Notion, helpdesk, etc.)?
5. If we fixed one workflow first, which one creates immediate value?

---

## Qualification Signals

Good fit when:

- Owner/manager feels operational pain now.
- Team has recurring workflow (weekly volume, not one-off).
- Buyer is open to pilot-first approach.

Low fit when:

- Buyer wants “AI experiment” with no business pain.
- No consistent process exists to improve.

---

## Offer Mapping

- One urgent workflow + low risk tolerance → **$199 Pilot**
- Ongoing need, small team → **Core ($397/mo)**
- Multi-channel/content throughput need → **Growth ($797/mo)**
- Multi-workflow + integration needs → **Scale ($1,497/mo)**

---

## Objection Handling

### “Too expensive”

“Totally fair. We can reduce scope instead of forcing price — for example, fewer monthly assets or one cadence batch.”

### “Need to think about it”

“No pressure. Easiest next step is pilot. Small scope, measurable result in 14 days, then decide with real data.”

### “Can AI do this for free?”

“Raw AI output is cheap. Reliable delivery, QA, integration, and consistency is what teams actually pay for.”

---

## Close Line

“Want me to send a one-page pilot scope today? If approved, we start this week and you’ll have first deliverable in a few days.”
