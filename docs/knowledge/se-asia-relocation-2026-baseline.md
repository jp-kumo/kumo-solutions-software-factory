# SE Asia Relocation Baseline (2026) — Property + Residency + Citizenship

> Working baseline for planning (not legal advice). Final decisions require country-specific legal counsel.

## High-confidence baseline

### Cambodia
- Foreigners generally **cannot directly own land**.
- Common legal structures: strata-title condo ownership, long-term lease, or structured ownership vehicles.
- Citizenship is possible via naturalization routes, including investment-linked pathways in some cases, but approval is discretionary and not an automatic “pay X, get passport” process.

### Philippines
- Foreigners generally **cannot directly own land**.
- Foreigners can own qualifying condominiums (subject to foreign ownership caps in condominium projects/corporations).
- Citizenship is possible via legal naturalization pathways; not a simple investor-passport route.

### Thailand
- Foreigners generally **cannot directly own land** (with narrow exceptions/structures).
- Foreigners can own qualifying condominiums (foreign quota rules apply).
- Long-stay/investment/work programs exist (e.g., LTR ecosystem), but citizenship remains a separate, high-friction process.

### Malaysia
- Foreigners can often purchase property under state-level minimum price rules and conditions.
- MM2H and related programs support long-term stay, but they are residency pathways, not direct citizenship.
- Citizenship remains separate and restrictive.

## Key correction to common assumption
- In SE Asia, “invest X and automatically get citizenship” is uncommon.
- Investor pathways usually provide visa/residency first; citizenship, if available, is later and conditional.
